What Belt is Jason Momoa in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu?

Publish date: 2024-06-16

By Keith Mado July 11, 2024 08:38

Photo by VCG/VCG VIA GETTY

One of the most acclaimed Hollywood actors, Jason Momoa, well-known for his role in "Game of Thrones" and as Aquaman, began training in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu (BJJ) in 2017. Inspired by his family and a circle of friends who are also BJJ practitioners, Momoa was eager to try something new. His passion for the sport made him train hard, directly reflecting his commitment to learn and adapt himself to BJJ techniques. Over the years, even with his busy acting practice, Momoa has remained committed to his BJJ training and has often incorporated it into his fitness routine.

Jason Momoa BJJ Belt and Accomplishments

Momoa has a true affection for the art, as he has been training BJJ and working on his skills since at least 2017. His current belt rank is white, but in BJJ, progress on the next colored belt level goes through demonstrated skill rather than just simple and specific requirements.

Inside Jason Momoa's BJJ Training And Techniques

Though the BJJ techniques and strategies of Jason Momoa are little known, it is clear that his tricks revolve around basic moves and core principles. Given that Momoa trains as a white belt, his practice perhaps focuses on mastering the basics: escapes of bad positions emphasizing mount, guard passing, and positional control. The demands Momoa faces in his acting roles may allow for elements of strength and conditioning to enhance his overall physical preparedness, given the nature he finds himself poised as a performer. His passion for the craft implies a fascination with getting better, something every BJJ practitioner needs to succeed.

Jason Momoa's attempt at Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu proves to be a true test for growth, fitness, and learning, which is challenging. Although he holds a white belt at the moment, Momoa's continuing progression in BJJ appears to capture what helps make jiu-jitsu beneficial: staying devoted and practicing together with the art of perseverance ingrained within it.
