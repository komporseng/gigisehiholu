Daniel Bryan

Daniel Bryan began his professional wrestling career in 1999 on the independent circuit, and signed an 18-month contract with the World Wrestling Federation (WWF, now WWE), in 2000; he later went on to make uncontracted appearances in WWE until 2003.

In 2002, he joined the Ring of Honor (ROH) promotion and competed in the main event of the company’s first event, The Era of Honor Begins. He was an ROH regular until 2009, winning the ROH World Championship and ROH Pure Championship once each, unifying them at one time, and becoming the original winner of the annual Survival of the Fittest tournament.

Moreover, he also competed extensively in Japan, earning the GHC Junior Heavyweight Championship in Pro Wrestling Noah and the IWGP Junior Heavyweight Tag Team Championship in New Japan Pro-Wrestling (alongside Curry Man). He has won two PWG World Championships, the FIP Heavyweight Championship, and the wXw World Heavyweight Championship once each on the independent circuit.

Returning to WWE

He rejoined WWE in 2009 but was fired for three months in 2010. In addition, he won the WWE Championship four times, the World Heavyweight Championship once, and the United States and Intercontinental Championships once each after his return.

He also won the WWE Tag Team Championship with Kane and the SmackDown Tag Team Championship with Erick Rowan, making him WWE’s 26th Triple Crown Champion and 15th Grand Slam Champion. In addition, he won the Money in the Bank ladder match in 2011.
